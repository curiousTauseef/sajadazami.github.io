About Me

I was born in 1995 in Tabriz, a historical city located in North-Western part of Iran. I was raised as a bilingual child since the province I was born in is Turkish and official language of the country is Farsi. In 2006 I was accepted into the NODET (National Organization for Developing Exceptional Talents) school. I spent seven years there until I was admitted to the Amirkabir University of Technology in Tehran at 2013. Living away from family in a dorm room in the 15 Million populated city of Tehran was a great experience, teaching me so many things about life.

I spent the first three semesters of my B.S., learning programming languages and software engineering concepts. I started with C and C++ and then I learned Java and Android programming and developed some applications in an experienced team. Later, I learned Python and some related packages.

I became familiar with AI world in Robotics Lab of the department supervised by Dr. Shiry, working on Multi-Agent Exploration and Human Detection System of robots in a 3D simulated environment(Gazebo).

From the beginning of my third year(5th semester), I started to learn about Machine Learning by watching online courses and reading books. Later I audited Machine Learning graduate course and took Statistical Machine Learning graduate course instructed by Dr. Nickabadi to gain a deeper understanding of ML concepts. We worked on various projects related to these fields and participated in some data mining competitions in Kaggle and DMC.

By the fourth year, we moved to Cognitive Robotics Lab of the department and I started working on Computer Vision problems using Deep Learning methods which lead to a publication on 2016. We also collaborated with Prof. Amigoni in AIRLab of Politecnico di Milano, Italy on a project related to simulation of robot communication and its impact on tasks like SLAM or exploration.

Since I used to read Philosophy and Psychology books/novels, I found Cognitive Science very exciting, so I tried to get into the field by watching videos lectures and reading more. Recently, I'm expanding my knowledge in Computational Neuroscience by reading books and papers and participating in Prof. Abdolhossein Abbasian's weekly long discussions in School of Cognitive Science(SCS) of The Institute for Research in Fundamental Sciences(IPM).

Currently, I'm working on three projects, A Novel System for Online Person Recognition of Home Robots(Computer Vision), A Communication Node for ROS-Gazebo Simulation(Robotics) and Corresponding ECoG, fMRI and EEG Signals in Human VTC in Royan Institue of Research collaborating with Prof. Khaligh-Razavi from MIT(Neuroscience, Deep Learning).
